# Louisiana Title Insurance Rates  

Louisiana’s title insurance rates are **filed and regulated by statute** (via the Louisiana Title Statistical Services Organization manual【64†L1-L9】). The current rate manual (effective Aug 1, 2024) provides the exact lender (loan) policy premium schedule and reissue (refinance) rules. Key points: 

- **Lender Policy Premiums:** Premiums are tiered by loan amount.  For example, for a standard loan policy: $100 premium per $1,000 for loans up to $12,000; $4.20 per $1,000 on $12,001–$50,000; $3.60 per $1,000 on $50,001–$100,000; $3.30 per $1,000 on $100,001–$500,000; then $2.70, $2.40, $2.10, $1.80, $1.50, $1.20 per $1,000 as amounts increase【76†L797-L802】. (All loans are rounded up to the next $1,000.)  

- **Refinance/Reissue Discount:** A refinancing borrower is entitled to a “reissue credit” of **60%** of the original rate (i.e. paying only 40% of the standard premium) if the property’s title was insured within 10 years【78†L37-L41】.  This rule (R-8 credit) applies to both owner’s and loan policies (60% factor of original rate)【78†L37-L41】.  

- **Common Endorsements:** The LATISSO manual lists all approved ALTA endorsements for Louisiana loans (e.g. standard ALTA 3, 4, 9 series, etc.) with their conditions【68†L110-L118】.  These endorsement premiums are also filed (fixed by statute).  (For instance, the manual shows the list of LA-specific ALTA 2006 forms and effective dates【68†L110-L118】.)  Providers typically use standard ALTA endorsements; specific required endorsements depend on transaction type (e.g. ALTA 3-06 “Zoning” or 9.1-06 “Residential Leaseholds” for residential loans, etc.).  

- **Closing/Notary Fees:** There is *no statewide published schedule* for settlement or notary fees in real estate closings. Notary fees in Louisiana are capped by law (typically a maximum of $75 for a mortgage act【47†L376-L381】), but actual title settlement fees are determined by local market/custom. We did not find an official “normed” fee – these are handled by closing agents or attorneys and vary.  

- **Regulation:** Title insurance premiums and endorsement charges are mandatory filed rates【64†L1-L9】. In contrast, closing and escrow fees are largely customary.  Notary fees are statutorily limited but not subject to voluntary filing. 

**Sources:** Louisiana DOI filings (LATISSO manual)【64†L1-L9】【76†L797-L802】, and state statutes.  *Last verified:* August 2024 (latest manual); checked May 2026. *Confidence:* **High** (official rate manual and regulations).
